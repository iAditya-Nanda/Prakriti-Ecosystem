# Future Scope: Prakriti Evolution Path

The Prakriti project is designed to be a scalable foundation for environmental sustainability. The following roadmap outlines the planned enhancements and strategic expansions for the platform.

## 1. Blockchain & Decentralization
- **Transition to PoS**: Shift from Proof-of-Work to a customized **Proof-of-Stake (PoS)** or **Proof-of-Authority (PoA)** to further reduce the energy footprint of the reward system.
- **Mainnet Integration**: Bridging the local Green Points ledger with public "Green Blockchains" (e.g., Celo, Algorand, or Polygon) to allow GP to be converted into globally recognized carbon credits.
- **Smart Contracts**: Implementation of self-executing contracts for environmental insurance and automated payout triggers for large-scale social cleanups.

## 2. Artificial Intelligence & IoT
- **IoT-Enabled "Smart Bins"**: Integration with hardware sensors in waste bins that automatically weigh and classify waste, verifying tasks without requiring manual photo uploads.
- **Satellite Reforestation Tracking**: Utilizing remote sensing and satellite data to verify tree-planting survival rates over years, rather than just the initial planting act.
- **Predictive Litter Analytics**: Using historical data to predict where littering is likely to occur (based on tourism seasons and weather) and alerting local authorities proactively.

## 3. Economic & Marketplace Expansion
- **Prakriti Marketplace-as-a-Service**: A dedicated e-commerce platform where users can spend GP directly on eco-friendly products (bamboo toothbrushes, solar chargers, etc.) from verified SMEs.
- **GP Staking & Eco-Investment**: Allow users to "stake" their points to fund local environmental startups or reforestation projects, earning a yield in the form of sustainability badges or tax benefits.
- **Carbon Footprint API**: Providing a public API for businesses to calculate and offset their carbon footprint using Prakriti's verified data.

## 4. Social & Community Gamification
- **Environmental "Clans"**: Allowing users to form neighborhood or university groups that compete in regional sustainability leagues.
- **AR (Augmented Reality) Eco-Guide**: An AR layer in the mobile app that shows users "hidden" eco-benefits (like the history of a tree or the impact of a nearby refill station) as they walk through a city.
- **Global Portability**: Enabling a user's "Green Score" and points to be valid across different states and countries, creating a global "Sustainability Passport."

## 5. Deployment & Scalability
- **Federated Node Network**: Deploying nodes at local municipalities and community centers to create a truly distributed and resilient regional network.
- **Offline-First Capabilities**: Enhancing the mobile app's ability to log tasks in remote "no-network" Himalayan zones using local mesh networking and delayed blockchain syncing.
